**RECITALS:**

A. The Founders are all of the shareholders of the Corporation.

B. The Founders intend this Agreement to be a **unanimous shareholder agreement** within the meaning of section 146 of the CBCA (a "USA"), and to restrict the powers of the directors to manage the business and affairs of the Corporation to the extent set out in Article 6.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Recitals & USA status.** This is the single most important structural upgrade from the research. There are two kinds of founder agreements under Canadian law: an *ordinary* shareholders' agreement (a private contract — binds the three of you as people, but legally **cannot** override the directors' authority to run the company), and a **Unanimous Shareholder Agreement ("USA")** under s.146 of the CBCA. Only a USA can lawfully pull management powers up from "the board decides" to "the shareholders decide" — which is exactly what your Article 6 unanimous-consent list does. Because all three of you are signing and you're all the shareholders, this agreement *qualifies* as a USA, and saying so makes your 2-of-3 rules and veto list actually enforceable, not just a handshake. Two consequences worth knowing: (1) a USA automatically binds anyone who later gets your shares (an estate, a buyer) without a separate signature — a safety net; (2) to the extent the USA moves a decision from directors to shareholders, it also moves the matching *legal responsibility* to the shareholders — but since the three of you are both the directors *and* the shareholders, this changes nothing real for you. Net: big upside, no practical downside.

## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

In this Agreement:

- **"Board"** means the board of directors of the Corporation.
- **"Cause"** means, with respect to a Founder: (a) fraud, theft, or embezzlement involving the Corporation; (b) intentional and material breach of this Agreement or of the Founder's confidentiality or IP obligations, which breach (if capable of remedy) remains unremedied 30 days after written notice describing it; (c) conviction of an indictable offence that materially harms the Corporation's business or reputation; or (d) carrying on, or being employed or engaged by, a business that directly competes with the Business, without the prior written consent of the other Founders. "Cause" does **not** include poor performance, reduced availability, disagreement in good faith, or resignation with Notice.
- **"Business"** means the development and commercialization of artificial-intelligence software products, including the voice-AI dispatch product currently branded "Dispango" and the document-AI product currently branded "CoverCraft", and any other business the Corporation carries on from time to time.
- **"Disability"** means a physical or mental condition that has prevented a Founder from performing substantially all of their duties to the Corporation for 6 consecutive months, or 9 months (whether or not consecutive) in any 18-month period.
- **"Fair Market Value" or "FMV"** means the value determined under the Valuation Mechanism in Article 8.
- **"Notice"** means at least 30 days' prior written notice to the Corporation and the other Founders.
- **"Shares"** means common shares in the capital of the Corporation, and any shares or securities into which they are converted, subdivided, or exchanged.
- **"Transfer"** means any sale, gift, assignment, pledge, encumbrance, or other disposition, direct or indirect.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 1.** The dictionary for the rest of the document. The one to read carefully is **"Cause"**: it's the list of serious misconduct that makes someone a "bad leaver" (worse buy-out terms). We kept it deliberately narrow — being busy, being wrong, or quitting politely is NOT "cause"; stealing, sabotage, or secretly working for a competitor is. The narrower this list, the harder it is for two founders to weaponize it against the third.

## ARTICLE 3 — REVERSE VESTING

3.1 **Vesting schedule.** Each Founder's Shares vest as to 1/48th of the total on the last day of each calendar month following the Vesting Start Date, such that all Shares are fully vested 4 years after the Vesting Start Date. There is no cliff.

3.2 **Vesting Start Date** means **April 13, 2026** (reflecting that substantial work on the Business began approximately three months before the date of this Agreement).

3.3 **Effect of ceasing to be active.** If a Founder ceases to be actively engaged in the Business (whether by resignation, removal, death, Disability, or otherwise) (the date of such cessation, the "Departure Date"), vesting stops on the Departure Date and:

  (a) the Corporation has the option, exercisable within 90 days of the Departure Date, to repurchase all of that Founder's **unvested** Shares at the original subscription price ($0.00001 per share); and

  (b) the departing Founder's **vested** Shares are dealt with under Article 4 (Leaver Provisions).

3.4 **Acceleration on acquisition (double trigger).** If (i) there is a sale of all or substantially all of the Corporation's shares or assets, and (ii) within 12 months after it, a Founder's engagement is terminated by the acquirer without Cause, then all of that Founder's unvested Shares vest immediately.

3.5 The Founders shall cause share certificates (or the securities register) to bear a legend referring to this Article.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 3.** You each own your million shares from day one — they vote and receive dividends immediately — but you *earn the right to keep them* month by month over 4 years (1/48th per month, no cliff, as agreed). Leave at month 12 and you keep 25%; the company can buy back the unearned 75% for pocket change. If the company is bought and the buyer then fires you, everything vests instantly — so an acquirer can't cheaply strip out a founder. The Vesting Start Date (3.2) is set to **April 13, 2026** — back-dated ~3 months to credit the work already done building Dispango, so each of you starts with roughly 6% already earned at signing rather than zero.

## ARTICLE 4 — LEAVER PROVISIONS

4.1 **Good Leaver** means a Founder whose engagement ceases due to: death; Disability; removal or termination by the Corporation without Cause; or resignation with Notice.

4.2 **Bad Leaver** means a Founder whose engagement ceases due to: termination for Cause; or resignation without Notice.

4.3 **Good Leaver terms.** A Good Leaver (or their estate) **keeps their vested Shares**, subject to the Transfer restrictions in Article 5. The Corporation and the remaining Founders additionally have the option (but not the obligation), exercisable within 90 days of the Departure Date, to purchase all or part of the Good Leaver's vested Shares at FMV.

4.4 **Bad Leaver terms.** The Corporation and the remaining Founders have the option, exercisable within 90 days of the Departure Date, to purchase all of a Bad Leaver's vested Shares at FMV. A Bad Leaver must cooperate with the exercise of this option.

4.5 **Payment terms.** Any purchase under this Article may, at the purchaser's election, be paid in up to 8 equal quarterly instalments over 24 months, with interest at the Bank of Canada policy rate plus 2% on the outstanding balance.

4.6 **Family-law event.** If, as a result of the breakdown of a Founder's marriage or common-law relationship, a spouse or former spouse of a Founder becomes entitled under family-law legislation or a court order to acquire, or acquires, any of that Founder's Shares, the Corporation first, then the other Founders pro-rata, have the option, exercisable within 90 days of receiving written notice of that entitlement, to purchase those Shares at FMV, payable under 4.5. Each Founder shall use reasonable efforts to ensure their Shares are not divided in specie to a spouse (for example, by satisfying any family-property entitlement out of other assets).

4.7 **Insolvency event.** If a Founder becomes bankrupt or insolvent, or their Shares become subject to seizure or a creditor's enforcement, the Corporation first, then the other Founders pro-rata, have the option, exercisable within 90 days, to purchase that Founder's Shares at **FMV** (not a discount), payable under 4.5. For certainty, this option is a purchase at fair value and is not intended to remove value from the Founder or their creditors.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 4.** What happens to the shares someone already earned when they leave. Leave honourably ("good leaver" — quit with 30 days' notice, get sick, get pushed out without misconduct) and your earned shares are yours to keep. Leave badly ("bad leaver" — fired for the serious-misconduct list, or ghost the company) and the others can force you to sell even your earned shares — but always at **fair market value set by an independent professional**, never at a punitive discount. The instalment option (4.5) means a buyout can't bankrupt the company in one cheque.
>
> Clauses 4.6 and 4.7 close two gaps the research flagged as standard on lawyers' checklists but missing from the first draft. **4.6 (divorce):** if a founder divorces, provincial family law can hand a chunk of that founder's shares to an ex-spouse — meaning you could suddenly co-own the company with someone's ex. This lets the company/other two buy those shares back at fair value so ownership stays with the working founders. **4.7 (insolvency):** if a founder goes personally bankrupt, their shares could fall into creditors' hands. Same fix — buy them back. **Important drafting detail:** a 2025 Alberta court decision (*ATB Financial v. Mayfield*) struck down a clause that grabbed a bankrupt shareholder's shares *cheaply*, because you can't use bankruptcy to strip value from creditors. So 4.7 is deliberately written to buy at *full fair value* — that's what keeps it enforceable.

## ARTICLE 5 — TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

5.1 **General prohibition.** No Founder may Transfer any Shares except in accordance with this Agreement and the Corporation's articles.

5.2 **Right of First Refusal (ROFR).** A Founder wishing to Transfer Shares to any person (a "Proposed Transfer") must first deliver written notice to the Corporation and the other Founders stating the number of Shares, the price, and the proposed transferee. The Corporation first, then the other Founders pro-rata, have 30 days to elect to purchase all (but not less than all) of those Shares on the same terms. Only if they decline may the Founder complete the Proposed Transfer, on terms no more favourable to the transferee, within 60 days, and only if the transferee first signs a joinder to this Agreement.

5.3 **Tag-along.** If a Founder proposes to Transfer Shares to a third party and the ROFR is not exercised, each other Founder may elect to sell a pro-rata portion of their own Shares to that third party on the same terms, and the Proposed Transfer may not complete unless the third party purchases the tagging Founders' Shares as well.

5.4 **Drag-along.** If Founders holding at least two-thirds of all issued Shares accept a bona fide arm's-length offer from a third party to purchase all of the issued Shares, they may require the remaining Founder(s) to sell on the same terms, provided that: (a) the price is not less than FMV determined under Article 8, or the offer follows a competitive process; (b) consideration is the same for all Founders per Share; and (c) the third party is not a person related to (within the meaning of the *Income Tax Act* (Canada)) any Founder voting in favour.

5.5 **Permitted exceptions.** Transfers under Articles 3, 4, or 7 (vesting repurchase, leaver buyout, death/disability) are not subject to the ROFR or tag-along.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 5.** Nobody can sell shares to an outsider behind the others' backs. Want out? The company and the other two get first dibs at the same price (**ROFR**). If a stranger does buy in, the others can join the sale on identical terms so no one is left behind (**tag-along**). And if two of the three of you accept a genuine offer for the whole company, the third can't block the sale (**drag-along**) — but only at an independently-verified fair price, and never a sweetheart deal to someone's cousin (5.4(c) blocks related-party drags).

## ARTICLE 6 — GOVERNANCE

6.1 **Board.** The Board consists of the three Founders for so long as each remains actively engaged in the Business. A Founder who ceases to be actively engaged (Article 3.3) is deemed to resign from the Board and from all offices of the Corporation effective on their Departure Date, and shall sign any documents needed to give effect to this, but retains the voting rights attached to any Shares they continue to hold as a shareholder.

6.2 **Ordinary decisions.** Except as set out in 6.3, all decisions of the Board and the shareholders are made by simple majority.

6.3 **Unanimous decisions.** The following require the written approval of all three Founders:

  (a) issuing shares, options, or securities convertible into shares to any Founder or any person related to a Founder;
  (b) amending the articles or by-laws of the Corporation, or this Agreement;
  (c) selling all or substantially all of the Corporation's assets or business, other than pursuant to Article 5.4 (drag-along);
  (d) any contract or payment between the Corporation and a Founder (or a person related to a Founder) other than compensation approved under 6.4, with a value over $5,000;
  (e) incurring indebtedness over $25,000 in the aggregate, or granting security over the Corporation's assets;
  (f) winding up or dissolving the Corporation; and
  (g) fundamentally changing the nature of the Business.

6.4 **Founder compensation.** Founder salaries, contractor fees, and bonuses are set by the Board by simple majority, provided that any change to a Founder's own compensation is decided without that Founder's vote counting.

6.5 **Dividends.** Dividends are declared by the Board by simple majority and are paid equally per Share. Nothing in this Agreement obliges the Corporation to declare a dividend.

6.6 **Pre-emptive rights.** Before issuing new Shares or securities to any person, the Corporation shall offer each Founder the right to purchase their pro-rata portion on the same terms, exercisable within 15 days. This right does not apply to issuances under an employee option plan approved under 6.3(a) or to a bona fide equity financing approved by the Board.

6.7 **Unanimous shareholder agreement.** The Founders declare this Agreement to be a unanimous shareholder agreement under section 146 of the CBCA. To that extent, and to the extent of the restrictions in 6.3, the power of the directors to manage the business and affairs of the Corporation is restricted, and the Founders, as shareholders, assume the corresponding rights, powers, duties, and liabilities of directors. This Agreement, and the rights and restrictions in it, bind every present and future holder of Shares, whether or not that holder signs a separate joinder.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 6.** Day-to-day: two votes out of three wins — the company can't stall. But a short list of dangerous moves needs all three signatures: printing new shares for an insider, changing the rules, selling everything, insider deals over $5k, big debt, shutting down. Your own pay is decided by the *other two* (6.4). Dividends stay majority-vote so cash can flow without drama (6.5). If new shares are ever issued, each founder can buy their proportional slice first so nobody gets silently diluted (6.6). And per 6.1: leave the business and you leave the boardroom — an ex-founder who keeps vested shares still votes those shares as a shareholder (e.g. ~16% if they left at year 2), but no longer sits on the Board running the company day-to-day.

## ARTICLE 7 — DEATH AND DISABILITY

7.1 On the death or Disability of a Founder, the Corporation first, then the remaining Founders pro-rata, have the option, exercisable within 120 days, to purchase all of the affected Founder's Shares (vested only; unvested Shares are repurchased under Article 3) at FMV.

7.2 Payment may be made in up to 8 equal quarterly instalments over 24 months, with interest at the Bank of Canada policy rate plus 2%.

7.3 The Founders agree to review, within 12 months of the Corporation achieving meaningful recurring revenue, whether to put in place corporate-owned life insurance on each Founder to fund the purchase in 7.1, and to revisit this Article with counsel at that time.

7.4 **Insurance proceeds excluded from value.** If the Corporation ever holds life or disability insurance on a Founder, the proceeds of that insurance are **not** included in determining the FMV of that Founder's Shares under Article 8. The proceeds are a source the Corporation may use to *pay* for the Shares, not an asset that increases their price.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 7.** If a founder dies or is long-term disabled, the others can buy the shares back at fair value — in instalments — so you never end up running the company with a grieving spouse or estate as an unwilling one-third owner (and they get fair money instead of illiquid shares). The proper long-term fix is life insurance that pays for this buyout; clause 7.3 forces the conversation once there's real revenue, instead of paying premiums now on empty pockets. Clause 7.4 is a subtle-but-important detail the research surfaced: if the company insures a founder's life and that founder dies, the insurance payout would (without this clause) briefly make the company "worth more" — inflating the price the company then has to pay the estate for the very same death. 7.4 says the payout is the *funding* for the buyout, not part of the price. (No revenue number is fixed in 7.3 — you'll judge "meaningful revenue" when you get there.)

## ARTICLE 8 — VALUATION MECHANISM

8.1 Wherever this Agreement requires Fair Market Value, it is determined by the first of the following that applies:

  (a) **Agreed Value certificate.** The Founders may, at any time (and are encouraged to do so at least annually, at the fiscal year-end), unanimously sign a written certificate stating the agreed value of the Corporation. If a certificate has been signed within the 12 months preceding the event requiring a valuation, the value it states (adjusted pro-rata for the Shares being valued) is the FMV, final and binding.

  (b) **Agreement at the time.** Failing (a), the interested parties attempt to agree a value in writing within 15 days.

  (c) **Independent valuation.** Failing (a) and (b), FMV is determined by a single Chartered Business Valuator (CBV), independent of all parties, jointly appointed by the parties or, failing agreement on the appointment within a further 15 days, appointed on application to the CBV Institute (or its successor). The CBV's written determination is final and binding, values the Shares as a going concern **without** minority or marketability discounts, and the CBV's fees are borne equally by the parties to the determination.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 8.** One pricing mechanism for every clause in this document that needs a share price — leaver buyouts, death, drag-along floors, deadlock exit. The cheap path (a): once a year, the three of you sign a one-line certificate — "we agree the company is worth $X" — and for the next 12 months that number settles any buyout automatically, no appraiser needed. This directly solves the "a small company's value is hard to calculate" problem: you set it yourselves while nobody's fighting. Only if there's no fresh certificate and no agreement does a professional appraiser (a Chartered Business Valuator) get hired as referee. "No minority discounts" means a one-third stake is worth a full one-third of the company — protecting whoever is being bought out. **Make the annual certificate a habit** (e.g. every year-end board meeting).

## ARTICLE 9 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND DEADLOCK

9.1 **Escalation ladder.** Any dispute under this Agreement, or any deadlock of the Board or shareholders, is resolved as follows: (a) good-faith negotiation among the Founders for 15 days; then (b) mediation before a single mediator in Calgary, Alberta for up to 30 days; then (c) final and binding arbitration by a single arbitrator in Calgary under the *Arbitration Act* (Alberta), with the arbitrator's costs following the outcome.

9.2 **Fundamental deadlock.** If a matter listed in 6.3 (unanimous matters) remains deadlocked after mediation, any Founder may serve a "Buy-Sell Notice" electing to sell all of their Shares, whereupon the Corporation and the other Founders have the option, for 60 days, to purchase those Shares at FMV under Article 8, payable under the instalment terms in 4.5. For clarity, no Founder can be **forced** to sell under this Article — it provides an exit at fair value, not an expulsion.

9.3 No Founder shall commence court proceedings except to enforce an arbitral award or seek urgent injunctive relief.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 9.** The anti-shotgun. Disputes go: talk → professional mediator → arbitrator whose ruling is final (faster and cheaper than court). If you're truly deadlocked on a big unanimous-list issue, any founder can choose to exit at independently-set fair value — but nobody can be *forced out* through this clause, and nobody's personal wealth gives them leverage. Compare that with a shotgun clause, where the richest founder wins by default.

## ARTICLE 10 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 **Assignment.** Each Founder hereby assigns to the Corporation all right, title, and interest in all intellectual property that the Founder creates, alone or jointly, that relates to the Business, whether created before or after the date of this Agreement — including the pre-incorporation work assigned under the *Pre-Incorporation IP Assignment Agreement* of even date, which the Founders confirm remains in effect.

10.2 **Moral rights.** Each Founder waives all moral rights in such intellectual property in favour of the Corporation.

10.3 **Confidentiality.** Each Founder shall keep confidential, and use only for the Business, all non-public information of the Corporation, during and after their involvement with the Corporation.

10.4 **Non-solicitation.** For 12 months after ceasing to hold Shares, a Founder shall not solicit the Corporation's employees or contractors to leave, or solicit its customers to move their business to a competing product, using the Corporation's confidential information.

10.5 **No broad non-compete.** The Founders deliberately do not impose a general non-competition covenant, relying instead on the definition of Cause (1), confidentiality (10.3), and non-solicitation (10.4).

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 10.** Everything any founder builds for the business belongs to the company — past (via the separate signing-day assignment document) and future. Departing founders can't raid the team or poach customers for a year, and can't use inside information ever. We deliberately skipped a full "you can't work in AI for 2 years" non-compete: Canadian courts rarely enforce those anyway, and the targeted protections above do the real work.

## ARTICLE 11 — GENERAL

11.1 **Term.** This Agreement terminates on the earlier of: written agreement of all Founders; one person becoming the sole holder of all Shares; or the closing of a bona fide equity financing or share sale in which it is superseded by a replacement shareholders' or investors' agreement.

11.2 **Governing law.** Alberta and the federal laws of Canada applicable in Alberta.

11.3 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Pre-Incorporation IP Assignment Agreement, is the entire agreement among the parties regarding its subject matter.

11.4 **Independent legal advice.** Each Founder acknowledges having had the opportunity to obtain independent legal advice before signing, and signs voluntarily.

11.5 **Amendment.** Only in writing, signed by all parties.

11.6 **Counterparts and electronic signatures.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, each of which is an original and all of which together form one agreement. The parties agree that electronic signatures (including e-signature platforms and scanned or PDF signatures) are valid, binding, and have the same effect as original signatures, consistent with the *Electronic Transactions Act* (Alberta) and the *Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act* (Canada).

11.7 **Effective date.** This Agreement is dated, and takes effect, as of the date the Corporation first issues Shares to the Founders under Article 2, so that the shareholding and this Agreement come into existence together.

> **PLAIN ENGLISH — Article 11.** Housekeeping. The agreement dissolves naturally when it's replaced by investor paperwork at a financing (11.1), so nothing here needs "unwinding" later. Clause 11.4 is why each of you should at least be *offered* the chance to have your own lawyer look at this — it makes the agreement much harder to attack later as unfair. **11.6:** you three don't need to be in the same room or print anything — electronic signatures are fully legal in Alberta and Canada for this kind of agreement, and "counterparts" means each of you can sign your own copy. **11.7:** the agreement should carry the *same date the shares are issued* — the two events are a matched pair, and dating them together avoids a gap where shares exist with no rules attached.
