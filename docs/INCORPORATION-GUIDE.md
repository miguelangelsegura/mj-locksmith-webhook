# Incorporation, Bank & GST — First-Timer Step-by-Step

**For:** Abdul (and the team) · **Goal:** stand up the legal company so we can sell safely.
Plain-language, one step at a time, with **cost + lead time** for each.

> **Not legal/tax advice.** These are current general figures (Canada, 2026) to get moving.
> Confirm the share structure and anything tax-related with an accountant before filing —
> it's cheap to get right now and expensive to fix later.

---

## The structure we're building

Incorporate **one parent company — a general AI company** — and run the locksmith
voice-agent as **a product/brand under it** (its own domain, e.g. Bengal/Dispango). Mechanics:

- The **corporation** is the legal entity that signs contracts, holds the bank account, and
  carries the liability.
- Each **product** is registered as a **trade name** ("operating as" / O/A) under that one
  corporation, and can have its own brand + domain.
- Day one this is simplest and cheapest: **one corp, multiple trade names.** If a product
  later gets big, you can spin it into its own subsidiary company — but you don't need that
  now.

**Decision needed before Step 1:** the **parent company name** (the general AI company),
separate from the "Dispango/Bengal" product brand.

---

## The steps

### Step 1 — Pick + check the parent company name
- Brainstorm the general-AI-company name. Decide **named** corporation (a real name) vs a
  **numbered** corporation (e.g. "1234567 Canada Inc." — you can add a trade name later).
- For a named federal corp, the name gets checked during online filing. Optionally buy a
  **NUANS pre-search** to confirm availability first.
- **Cost:** $0 (name search is built into online filing) or ~$14–$80 for an optional NUANS
  pre-search. **Lead time:** an afternoon of deciding.

### Step 2 — Incorporate federally
- File **Articles of Incorporation** at the Corporations Canada **Online Filing Centre**.
- You'll need: director info (**at least 25% of directors must be Canadian residents**), a
  registered office address, and a **share structure** (how shares split between Abdul +
  Miguel — confirm this with an accountant first).
- **Cost:** **$200** online. **Lead time:** **~1 business day** (or 4 hours with paid express).
- **You receive:** Certificate of Incorporation, the corporation key, and a **Business Number
  (BN)** from CRA automatically.

### Step 3 — File the Individuals with Significant Control (ISC) register
- A legal requirement: within **30 days** of incorporating, file info on anyone owning/
  controlling **25%+** of shares (that's both founders). Done in the Online Filing Centre.
- **Cost:** $0. **Lead time:** minutes. **Don't skip it — it's mandatory and dated.**

### Step 4 — Register the product trade name(s) ("operating as")
- Register **"Dispango"/the locksmith brand** as a trade name under the corporation in Ontario,
  so the product can brand + bank under its own name while the corp is the legal entity.
- **Cost:** **$60** (valid 5 years). **Lead time:** same day, online.

### Step 5 — Set up CRA tax accounts (GST/HST)
- Open a **GST/HST account** under the BN (it becomes `BN + RT0001`). Mandatory once worldwide
  taxable sales exceed **$30,000** in any 4 consecutive quarters — but you can register
  **early/voluntarily** (lets you claim input tax credits and looks established).
- Ontario HST is **13%**.
- **Cost:** $0. **Lead time:** can be immediate online.

### Step 6 — Open the business bank account
- Bring: **Certificate of Incorporation + Articles + BN + photo ID** (both directors usually).
- **Cost:** usually a small monthly account fee. **Lead time:** same day to a few days
  (some banks need an in-branch appointment).

### Step 7 — Business insurance (don't skip, given what we do)
- Get a quote for **general liability + errors & omissions (E&O)**. We handle people's
  emergency calls and customer data, so this matters.
- **Cost:** varies (often ~$50–$150/mo for a small tech/services co). **Lead time:** a few days
  to quote/bind.

### Step 8 — Bookkeeping hygiene
- Run **everything** through the company account, keep receipts, and keep personal and company
  money separate from day one (see the next section on why this matters).

## Lead-time / cost summary

| Step | Cost | Lead time |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Name decision (+ optional NUANS) | $0 (or ~$14–$80) | an afternoon |
| 2. Federal incorporation | $200 | ~1 business day |
| 3. ISC filing | $0 | minutes (within 30 days) |
| 4. Trade name (O/A) registration | $60 | same day |
| 5. GST/HST account | $0 | immediate–1 day |
| 6. Business bank account | small monthly fee | same day–few days |
| 7. Business insurance | ~$50–$150/mo | few days |

**Bottom line:** the legal entity itself is **~$260 and ~1–2 business days** of actual
processing. The bank account + insurance add a few more days.

## Can we start selling before the company bank account exists?

**Short answer: yes — once incorporated — and it does insulate us, *if we operate as the
company.*** Details:

- **The shield turns on at incorporation**, not when the bank account opens. The corporation
  is a separate legal person the moment the Certificate is issued. Liability protection comes
  from **acting as the company** — sign contracts and invoice in the **company's legal name**,
  and sign "on behalf of [Company] Inc." So we can do outreach, demos, and even sign customers
  in the company's name the day after incorporating, before the bank account is open.

- **The risk is the money, not the contracts.** Running customer payments through a *personal*
  account "commingles funds" and can weaken the corporate veil (a court could argue the company
  isn't truly separate). It also creates a tax/bookkeeping mess.

- **If you must take money before the business account opens (keep it short and clean):**
  1. Invoice in the **company name** (and quote GST/HST once registered).
  2. Treat any funds received personally as **company money held in trust** — don't spend it.
  3. **Transfer it in full to the company account** the moment that account opens, with a clear
     record ("customer X payment, received [date], deposited to company [date]").
  4. Keep the window as short as possible.
  - **Better:** open the business account ASAP (it's fast once you have the Certificate + BN),
    or set up **Stripe under the company** and just point payouts at the company account once
    it exists — Stripe can be configured before the first charge.

- **Net:** incorporate first → you're insulated for liability and can sell in the company name
  immediately; just don't let revenue live in a personal account any longer than unavoidable,
  and document every dollar. Confirm specifics with an accountant.
